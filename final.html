If the US stock market falls, will the Korean stock market also fall?¶

Joon Kwak¶

Introduction¶

Koreans have long said that when US stocks rise, Korean stocks rise, and when US stocks fall, Korean stocks fall. It is because Korea has a close relationship with the United States in trade, economy, and politics. The volume of bilateral trade between Korea and the United States reached 135.2 billion dollars in 2019, a significant increase of more than 34 percent, 100.8 billion dollars in 2011, before the conclusion of the Korea-US FTA. The United States is Korea's second largest trading partner, and Korea is the US's sixth largest trading partner.

Oh! So, can anyone get rich by trading Korean stocks compared to the US stock index? Can the US stock index really predict the Korean stock index?

In this tutorial, I will introduce the relationship between stock indices using Pearson's correlation coefficient, machine learning algorithms and analysis, and the skills needed to do this, which will lead me to conclusions on my topic.

Correlation coefficient display¶

The correlation coefficient described above can be output using the corr() function of the pandas data frame. Here, the Pearson correlation coefficient is used. What is Pearson's correlation coefficient? The Pearson correlation coefficient (Pearson's r) is commonly used to find the correlation between two variables.

The value of r is +1 if X and Y are exactly the same, 0 if they are completely different, and -1 if they are exactly the same in the opposite direction.

The coefficient of determination is calculated as r^2, which means the degree to which Y can be predicted from X.

In general, it is interpreted as follows.

  • If r is between -1.0 and -0.7, then there is a strong negative linear relationship
  • If r is between -0.7 and -0.3, then there is a pronounced negative linear relationship
  • If r is between -0.3 and -0.1, a weakly negative linear relationship
  • If r is between -0.1 and +0.1, a linear relationship that can be almost neglected
  • If r is between +0.1 and +0.3, then there is a weak quantitative linear relationship
  • If r is between +0.3 and +0.7, then there is a clear quantitative linear relationship
  • If r is between +0.7 and +1.0, then there is a strong quantitative linear relationship

For more information, visit pandas.Dataframe.corr Pearson correlation coefficient

Hypothesis Testing and Machine Learning¶

A hypothesis test evaluates two mutually exclusive statements about a population to determine which statement is best supported by the sample data.

Null Hypothesis¶

The null hypothesis states that there is no relationship between the two variables being studied. One variable does not affect the other. It states the results are due to chance and are not significant in terms of supporting the idea being investigated. Thus, the null hypothesis assumes that whatever you are trying to prove did not happen.

P-value¶

A p-value, or probability value, is a number describing how likely it is that the data would have occurred by random chance. The level of statistical significance is often expressed as a p-value between 0 and 1. The smaller the p-value, the stronger the evidence that you should reject the null hypothesis. A p-value less than 0.05 is statistically significant. It indicates strong evidence against the null hypothesis, as there is less than a 5% probability the null is correct. Therefore, we reject the null hypothesis, and accept the alternative hypothesis.

Paired sampled t-test¶

The paired sample t-test is also called dependent sample t-test. It’s an uni variate test that tests for a significant difference between 2 related variables.

Cross-Validation (k-Fold Cross Validation)¶

Just because you've created a model and made predictions doesn't mean the model makes good predictions. Sometimes you get good marks in training, but you get very bad marks in training. This is called overfitting. To avoid this, we don't train the entire training data set at once, but leave some and use it for testing. This method is called Cross-Validation.

It first divides the training dataset into k randomly by units called Folds. This is used to increase the reliability of performance measurements when the amount of data is insufficient. Evaluating estimator performance is a method of finding the optimal model parameter.

When k=5, i.e., after splitting the data into 5 subsets, one fold for each split is used for testing and the remaining four folds are used for training. Repeat this process to measure the accuracy for each division.

For more information, visit cross_validation and cross_val_score

Conclusion¶

The result of finding the Pearson correlation coefficient of the Korean stock market and the American stock market was very high. From the Cross-Validation, we can see that it can be seen that the Korean stock market and the US stock market are closely related.

This shows that the Korean stock market is closely related to the American stock market. However, since this analysis is based only on data from 2020, the results may be different if past data are included. In addition, there are many factors other than the US stock market that determine the stock price of the KOSPI. Therefore, it may be risky to invest in the KOSPI by comparing only the US stock market with the Korean stock market.

In the future, if you compare the stock markets of other countries as well as Korea and the US, interesting results may be obtained. It is also worth exploring other methods besides the linear regression used in this tutorial.
